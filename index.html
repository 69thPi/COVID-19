::On the chemical confirmation of presence of RNA.
The small quantity of taget RNA in the test buffer "B" causes the detection to be insignificant. Hence, Bial's Orcinol test and similar reaction that involve the nucleic acid (RNA here) are defunt here.
Instead the approach taken was that of a transistor in a common Em. config. acting as a switch. Where the small quantity of base current can be used to carry out a reaction. In chemical terms use use the RNA as a catalyst that allows a color change reaction.
Q> How do we cause "visible" color change?
Color percieved is of complimentary nature to the wavelengths absorbed by the outermost bonding and lone electrons in a molecule. This energy absorbed is used for excitation to anti-bonding orbitals from certain energy levels. Usally this energy gap corresponds to the UV range hence rendering the molecule as colorless. The Energy gap decreases with the delocalization of the electrons in a molecule and hence they absorb lower energy wavelengths; entering into the visble spectra.

So what we do here is to use the RNA to cause a reaction that increases the delocalization of a molecule hence imparting color.
If any target RNA is present in the test buffer "B" it will cause the colorless buffer to render in a colored one.
